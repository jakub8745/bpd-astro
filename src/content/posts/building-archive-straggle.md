Building the Blue Point Art Gallery archive seemed simple on the surface. The programming is not that difficult. Especially as we have access to interesting tools that can speed up the work. However, there are times when I am completely stumped. 

I don't like stagnation. 

A month ago I thought it might be a good idea to rebuild the static elements of the archive, the exhibition rooms, in Blender and bake the textures. In theory this would improve performance.  But baking textures in Blender turned out to be a nightmare for me. I baked the 'WakeUp Call' exhibit for 3 weeks. The Lockdowns exhibit took 4 days. I'm not fond of both. I'm saving them for future revisions. The next exhibitions will be more complex and complicated. I'm learning to be more patient. 

then I want to introduce 'compressed textures'. And here I see another problem related to the 'texture atlas', the UV map etc. 

I'm an 'army of one' and it's a bit cumbersome to cover all the topics and tasks by myself.
